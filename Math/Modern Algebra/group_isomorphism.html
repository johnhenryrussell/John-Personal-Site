Isomorphisms of Groups

Two groups \((G, *\)) and \((H, \circ)\) are isomorphic is there exists a bijection \(\phi: G \rightarrow H\) such that \[\phi(a * b) = \phi(a) \circ \phi(b)\] for all \(a,b \in G\)

Problem: Prove that the groups \((2 \mathbb{Z}, +)\) and \((\mathbb{Z}, +)\) are isomorphic.

Proof:

Define the function \(\phi:\mathbb{Z} \rightarrow 2\mathbb{Z}\) by \(\phi(n) = 2n\) for all \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\). We will first show that \(\phi\) is a bijection.
