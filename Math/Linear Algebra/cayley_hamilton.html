The Cayley Hamilton Theorem

Let \(A \in M_{n \times n}(F)\). The determinant of \(A\) is defined as \[\det(A) = \sum_{j=1}^n (-1)^{1+j} A_{1j} \cdot \det(\tilde{A}_{1j}) \] where \(\tilde{A}_{ij}\) is the matrix \(A\) with row \(i\) and column \(j\) deleted and \(M_{n \times n}(F)\) is the set of \(n \times n\) matrices with entries in \(F\).

Let \(A \in M_{n \times n}(F)\). The inverse of \(A\) is the matrix \(A^{-1}\) such that \[A A^{-1} = \mathcal{I_n} \] where \(\mathcal{I_n}\) is the \(n \times n\) indentity matrix.

Let \(A \in M_{n \times n}(F)\). The polynomial \(f(t) = \det(A - t I_n)\) is called the characteristic polynomial of \(A\).

Let \(A\) be an \(n \times n\) matrix, and let \(f(t)\) be the characteristic polynomial of \(A\). Then \(f(A) = \mathcal{O}\), the \(n \times n\) zero matrix.

Problem: Let \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 1&2&3\\7&3&5\\-6&4&2 \end{pmatrix} \). Use the Cayley-Hamilton theorem to find \(A^{-1}.\)

We will begin by finding the characteristic polynomial of \(A\).

\[ \begin{align} f(t) &= \det(A - tI_n) = \det \begin{pmatrix} (1-t)&0&0\\7&(3-t)&5\\-6&4&(2-t) \end{pmatrix}\\ \\ \\ &= (1-t) \det \begin{pmatrix} (3-t)&5\\4&(2-t) \end{pmatrix} - 2 \det \begin{pmatrix} 7&5\\-6&(2-t) \end{pmatrix} + 3\begin{pmatrix} 7&(3-t)\\-6&4 \end{pmatrix} \\ \\ \\ &=(1-t)(t^2-5t-14) - 2(-7t + 44) + 3(-6t + 46)\\ \\ \\ f(t) &=\color{red} -t^3 + 6t^2 + 5t + 36 \end{align} \]

The Cayley-Hamilton theorem tells us that

\[f(A) = -A^3 + 6A^2 + 5A + 36 \, \mathcal{I} = \mathcal{O} \].

Solving for \(\mathcal{I}\), we get

\[ \begin{align} \mathcal{I} &= \frac{1}{36}(A^3-6A^2-5A)\\ \\ &=A \bigg(\frac{A^2-6A -5}{36}\bigg) \end{align} \]

Notice that the above equation is of the form \(AA^{-1} = \mathcal{I} \). Thus, by Def. 2

\[A^{-1} = \bigg(\frac{A^2-6A -5}{36}\bigg)\]

Lastly, we can plug \(A\) into the above equation to get our inverse.

\[ \mathbf{A^{-1}=\begin{pmatrix} -\frac{7}{18}&\frac{2}{9}&\frac{1}{36}\\ -\frac{11}{9}&\frac{5}{9}&\frac{4}{9}\\ \frac{23}{18}&-\frac{4}{9}&-\frac{11}{36} \end{pmatrix}} \]
